About Me

Hello, I'm Corrinne Schultze. I am graduate from the University of Central Florida who majored in Digital Media and currently work for the Techrangers at UCF's Center for Distributed Learning. My focus is on web design and development, but I also enjoy traditional art. My strengths lie in front-end design with HTML and CSS, but I am forever expanding my knowledge of scripting and programming. I also enjoy practicing digital art and design, and am always willing to try a new medium.

I decided to major in digital media because technology has been an important part of my life. I grew up with video games in my home and bonded with my brother and cousins by playing these games together, and in turn, all sharing the same interests. As a child, my parents had an Atari 2600 and a Nintendo Entertainment System. Ever since, I have been on the brink of new console and game releases, and have also expanded my interests to computer games.

After having a hand-me-down Macintosh computer for years, my family finally bought our first computer in 2001. Immediately, I was hooked, and began catching up on the wonderful world of games and technology that I had missed. With this computer, we also finally had Internet access, and my life in the revolutionary technologic world kept changing.

By the time I made it to high school, I was taking beginning web design and programming classes, and have been hooked since. For years I have been practicing HTML, CSS, Javascript, and more, and continue to expand my knowledge by learning more technologies, scripting languages, and programming languages. I love to draw and utilize physical mediums as well. I get a thrill out of experimenting with new media, both on and off the computer.

I enjoy a lot of physical and hands-on activities, so I'm constantly moving to keep myself active and entertained. I spend a lot of my time designing, doing arts & crafts, playing video games, baking, and updating my blog, Galactic Lacquer. I love technology and enjoy it using both for work and for leisure.
